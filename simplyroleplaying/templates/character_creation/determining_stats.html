When creating a character, each player gets a pool of 12 character creation points, unless otherwise specified by their respective race. They have six basic stats to spend these points in: Agility, Brawn, Charisma, Intuition, Knowledge, and Luck.

Each of these stats are described in more detail below. After you have finished reading through the details of each stat, distribute your 12 points into the stats you feel your character would be best at. Initially, each character may have a maximum of 4 points invested in each stat. The amount of points invested in each stat directly correlates to the proficiency your character has in those stats.

Non-Dice Based Stats

Wounds: Your character can only take a certain number of damage from any source before they die. In the case of this system, the damage is measured in wounds. Wounds are caused in many different ways; your Gamemaster will tell you when you have accrued wounds as a result of your actions. The maximum amount of wounds your character can take is based on your Brawn score (see below). If your character sustains more wounds than their maximum, they go unconscious and enter a state of "bleeding out". Every subsequent turn after, your character takes an additional wound. If at any point your character reaches a total number of wounds equal to 150% of their maximum wound level, they immediately die.

Exhaustion: Everyone can get tired out, even characters in a fictional realm. Your character will take Exhaustion damage whenever they do anything too strenuous, which (if they exceed their maximum Exhaustion points) will lead to them passing out, unconcious. The maximum amount of Exhaustion points your character can take is based on your Agility score (see below).

Agility

Agility is, primarily, to determine just how fast one is on their feet, though it also governs reaction time. One of the most common uses for Agility is for feats of speed and dexterity in combat, such as dodging and hitting. Agility is also used to calculate the total amount of Exhaustion points your character can spend before passing out, using the following formula: 12+A where A is the maximum roll that you could roll on an Agility check. You can recover Exhaustion points by resting.

Example skills that could use Agility:

  • Picking a lock
  • Recovering from a fall
  • Making a jump
  • Avoiding a trap

Brawn

Brawn governs how strong and sturdy a character is, or how many hits they can take and how much damage they dole out. Your Brawn is also used to calculate the total amount of Wounds your character can take before passing out, and is based on the following formula: 12+B where B is the maximum roll that you could roll on a Brawn check. You can recover Wound points by resting or being healed. In addition, B is the maximum number of wounds beyond the maximum that a character can take before dying.

When a character passes their maximum wound level, they will usually begin to "bleed out" every turn from sustained injuries. Each round, the character must make an increasingly difficult Endurance check, or recieve one wound. This process of bleeding out continues until the character is stabilized, which is usually acchieved though healing magic or bandages.

Example skills that could use Brawn:

  • Pulling someone up a cliff
  • Breaking a door
  • Swimming long distance
  • Running long distance

Charisma

Charisma primarily deals with how well liked a character can make themselves in a social scenario. Charisma also affects how attractive others find you, and your mental fortitude.

Example skills that could use Charisma:

  • Convincing someone to give you a discount
  • Intimidating a mugger to leave you alone
  • Bluff in a card game
  • Charm someone with your beguiling looks

Intuition

Intuition is your mental quickness. Think of it as Agility, but for your mind. It is primarily used for magic in combination with Knowledge, but can also be used to keep a good eye on your surroundings.

Example skills that could use Intuition:

  • Spotting a glimmering sword in a wreck
  • Discovering something hidden
  • Disarming a trap
  • Determining how a mechanism works

Knowledge

Knowledge is your mental Brawn. Knowledge comprises of what you know, and how well you know it. Knowledge is also the prime skill for all things magical, along with Intuition.

Example skills that could use Knowledge:

  • How a foreign culture view certain objects
  • Discerning the origins of an item, based on the craftsmanship
  • Building mechanisms
  • Remembering details

Luck

Luck is both the best and the worst skill in the game. It is used whenever the gamemaster chooses to, so put points in it as a safety net in case your other skills fail. Ultimately, Luck can be all that stands between you and certain death.
